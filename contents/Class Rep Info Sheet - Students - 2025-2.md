## Page 1

You have volunteered as a Class Representative, now what? 
• 
Register through the UCSA website: www.ucsa.org.nz/support/classrepresentatives/ under the rele-
vant course or year-level code e.g. LAWS101 or ENME400. 
• 
Introduce yourself to your classmates, by whatever means will be most effective for you. You could 
stand up and introduce yourself in-class, make a Facebook group, ask your lecturer to put your details 
on Learn, or send an introductory email to classreps@ucsa.org.nz to be sent to your class, 
• 
Complete the Class Rep training. Details of the training are sent out to Class Reps who have signed 
up through the UCSA website. Let us know if you have done the training previously so we can mark 
you as completed. You may have to repeat the training if there have been any major changes. 
• 
Provide feedback to your Department / School, either by email to your course coordinator or admin-
istrator, or by attending Departmental meetings. Your Department may have particular ways it prefers 
to receive feedback, so check with your course coordinator or administrator. 
• 
Complete the end of semester online survey, which will be sent to you by email. 
Class Representative Responsibilities: 
• 
Assisting other students in your class with academic issues impacting their studies. Examples of issues 
you can assist fellow students with are: conflicting due dates for assessments, issues with a lecturer 
etc. You are there to help resolve issues that affect the majority of the class at the lowest level. 
• 
Improve and streamline communication between the students in your class and the UC staff who 
teach that class. 
• 
Providing feedback on courses to the relevant course coordinators, and Department / School to im-
prove courses for future students. 
• 
Providing the UCSA with feedback about the student experience at UC. This helps the UCSA identify 
and resolve repeated issues and make UC a better place to study. 
Rewards 
• 
Chance to win one of ten $50 Westfield vouchers. 
You should not deal with: 
• 
Harassment and serious conflict; 
• 
Student-to-student conflict; 
• 
Ongoing one-on-one advocacy; or 
• 
Students’ personal problems. 
You are also not expected to: 
• 
Share your notes (unless you want to); 
• 
Provide tutoring to students; 
• 
Put up with abusive emails/comments; or 
• 
Proof-read other students’ assignments.
